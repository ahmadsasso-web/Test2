1A. Creating Your Financial Project

This is where you define your workspace. Name it clearly, e.g., "Q4 2024 Inventory Analysis" or "APAC Fixed Asset Review."

1B. Importing Documents

Upload your financial reports (PDFs, spreadsheets, internal memos, etc.). This content is private and the only data the AI will use.

  • **Content Limit:** You can add up to **300 sources** per notebook.
  • **Best Practice:** Upload related documents, such as all regional Accounts Payable reports or all Fixed Asset schedules, to a single notebook for synthesis.

Configure Notebook: Setting AI Preferences

Use the 'Configure' setting (usually near the chat bar) to set rules for the AI's responses. This is critical for finance use cases. For example, you can instruct the AI to:

  • **Be concise and formal:** "Always respond in a concise, professional tone, focusing on quantifiable metrics."
  • **Use specific terminology:** "When discussing Fixed Assets, strictly use IFRS terminology."

Step 2: Verify & Extract from Single Source

Use the tools in the **'Studio' panel in the right hand side** on an individual source to validate the AI's understanding. This is essential for validating the AI has understood your source document and for getting rapid summaries/videos/audio overviews from a large report without reading it page by page.

Summarize (Quick Audit)

Get a high-level summary of this document's scope, key figures, and conclusions. Useful for auditing reports or preparing for a review meeting. This **validates** that the AI correctly understood the document's main content.

Ask Questions (Metric Extraction)

Ask specific, complex questions *about this single file* to **extract** metrics. Examples:

  • "What is the total accumulated depreciation for Fixed Assets in this report?"
  • "Calculate the inventory turnover ratio based on the numbers provided."

Generate Themes (Focusing Analysis)

The AI will identify key themes like "Capital Expenditure Projections" or "Accounts Payable Aging." This helps you quickly confirm the document covers the topics you need.

Step 3: Chat and interact with the AI

This is the most powerful part. Use the **main chat bar** (labeled "Ask the Notebook") to analyze data *across* all your uploaded documents. This allows for complex comparisons and cross-referencing that would be time-consuming to do manually.

Ask the Notebook...

Step 5: Sharing and Collaboration

Once your notebook is set up, you can share it with team members, much like sharing a Google Sheet or Slide deck. This allows multiple people to leverage the synthesized data and extracted insights simultaneously.

Editor Access

Grant Editor access to team members who need to actively build the notebook or perform their own custom analysis.

  • Can **View and Interact** with the main chat.
  • Can **View all Sources** and the **Studio** panel.
  • Can **Add, Remove, or Replace** source documents.
  • Can **Pin** new notes.

Viewer Access

Grant Viewer access to stakeholders (e.g., leadership) who only need to consume final insights or ask high-level questions.

  • Can **View and Interact** with the main chat.
  • Can only **Communicate** with the AI based on existing sources.
  • **Cannot** view sources or use the Studio panel tools.
  • **Cannot** add, remove, or modify sources or notes.

Step 6: Best Practices & Workflow Summary

To ensure accurate and reliable financial analysis, adhere to these practices. The AI is a tool; your expertise is the driving force.

Garbage In, Garbage Out

The quality of the AI's responses is entirely dependent on the quality and relevance of your source financial documents.

Always Verify

Treat the AI's output as a *draft*. Always check the citations against the raw financial data to confirm accuracy, especially when presenting final figures.

Be Specific

Ask focused, metric-driven questions, e.g., "What is the weighted average cost of inventory for Q3 2024?" not just "Tell me about inventory."
